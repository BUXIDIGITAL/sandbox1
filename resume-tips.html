Expert Resume Tips

Learn how to create a resume that gets you noticed by recruiters

1

Tailor Your Resume to Each Job

One size doesn't fit all when it comes to resumes. Customize your resume for each position by:

  • Reading the job description carefully and identifying key requirements
  • Highlighting relevant skills and experiences that match the role
  • Using keywords from the job posting in your resume
  • Emphasizing achievements that align with the company's needs
2

Use Action Verbs and Quantify Achievements

Make your accomplishments stand out by starting bullet points with strong action verbs and including specific numbers:

  • Instead of: "Responsible for managing social media"
  • Write: "Increased social media engagement by 150% through strategic content planning"
  • Action verbs to use: Achieved, Implemented, Spearheaded, Optimized, Generated, Streamlined
  • Include metrics: percentages, dollar amounts, time saved, team size
3

Keep It Concise and Relevant

Recruiters spend an average of 6-7 seconds scanning a resume. Make every word count:

  • Aim for 1 page if you have less than 10 years of experience
  • 2 pages maximum for senior professionals
  • Remove outdated or irrelevant information
  • Focus on the last 10-15 years of experience
  • Use bullet points instead of paragraphs
4

Optimize for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)

Many companies use ATS software to screen resumes. Ensure yours passes through:

  • Use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
  • Avoid tables, text boxes, and complex formatting
  • Include relevant keywords from the job description
  • Use a simple, clean font (Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman)
  • Save as PDF unless otherwise specified
5

Write a Compelling Professional Summary

Your summary is your elevator pitch. Make it count:

  • Keep it to 2-3 sentences or 50-100 words
  • Highlight your most relevant qualifications
  • Include your years of experience and key expertise
  • Mention your career goals if changing industries
  • Example: "Results-driven Marketing Manager with 8+ years of experience in digital strategy and brand development. Proven track record of increasing ROI by 200% through data-driven campaigns. Seeking to leverage expertise in a senior leadership role."
6

Highlight Relevant Skills

Create a dedicated skills section that showcases your abilities:

  • Include both hard skills (technical) and soft skills (interpersonal)
  • List skills that match the job requirements
  • Be honest - only include skills you actually possess
  • Consider organizing by category (Technical, Languages, Certifications)
  • Update regularly as you learn new skills
7

Proofread Thoroughly

Typos and grammatical errors can instantly disqualify you:

  • Read your resume out loud to catch errors
  • Use spell-check tools but don't rely on them alone
  • Ask a friend or mentor to review it
  • Check for consistency in formatting, dates, and punctuation
  • Verify all contact information is correct
8

Include Relevant Certifications and Education

Showcase your qualifications and continuous learning:

  • List your highest degree first
  • Include relevant certifications and licenses
  • Add graduation dates if you're a recent graduate
  • Mention honors, awards, or relevant coursework
  • Include professional development and training

Using an Unprofessional Email

Use a simple, professional email address (firstname.lastname@email.com)

Including Personal Information

Don't include age, marital status, photo (in US), or social security number

Listing Job Duties Instead of Achievements

Focus on what you accomplished, not just what you were responsible for

Using Generic Objectives

Skip the objective or make it specific and value-focused

Inconsistent Formatting

Maintain consistent fonts, spacing, and bullet point styles throughout

Lying or Exaggerating

Always be truthful - lies will be discovered and can end your career
